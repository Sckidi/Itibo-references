QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

Introduce your business and what you do there.

The company is SequenceBase Corporation. We produce a database of worldwide genetic patented genes. Users are generally from drug development companies, biotech companies, etc. I’m president and CEO.


What challenge were you trying to address with Azati?

We needed a comprehensive website and portal to use as part of our ongoing business.


What was the scope of their involvement?

Azati developed our website, which is a research portal. They put together data from 20 to 25 different sources. They put it in a form where users would be able to read and manipulate it very readily. In addition to that, in order to search genetic sequences, there’s an algorithm that’s used. They were able to develop additional algorithms that work very fast and more accurately than what we had before. Our users seem to love it. When a potential customer comes to the site, we can put together a trial and show them what we offer. They can try it out and will hopefully sign up for a subscription. Once they have the subscription, they’re able to search genetic sequences and see if they’ve been patented anywhere in the world. They can export the report, manipulate it, add additional generic profiles to it, and then enhance the report or whatever they want. This is a difficult subject dealing with bioinformatics, or more specifically genetics, DNA, nucleotides, and proteins, and not many people know this area of expertise. I was happy with Azati because they were able to step up to the plate and put everything together for me in a pretty good fashion.


What is the team composition?

I had a previous business called Patent Value Predictor that they developed a web portal for. For that one, we had an algorithm that would put a value on a patent based on certain parameters. We were pleased with the work they did for that portal and, when I started SequenceBase, I knew who to turn to. I knew I could give them a complex project that very few people would have a background in and they would be able to create a knowledge base and move forward fairly quickly. I found them easy to work with.
